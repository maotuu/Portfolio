From 2013 to 2015, I worked at QSC, the student portal of Zhejiang University. As the design lead, I and my team designed products and services to provide thousands of students the ultimate campus experience. In 2014, we handled a product audit of our main four products and redesigned Mobile, the core product of us.

RESEARCH

#1. Pain Points

Cumbersome websites cause frustration.

Students in Zhejiang University are frustrated with the cumbersome process of accessing many different websites to obtain the information that they need for school. Courses, exams, homework, labs, school bus information and exam date are all at different places. They either waste time checking all of them, or get lost.

Students miss fascinating on-campus events.

With Renren (the social network once lauched as “China’s Facebook”) reaching its vanishing point, the most important channel to spread on-campus events was cut off.
Well, some people say that, "You still have Wechat! Students share Moments (Wechat's social network posts). Since most Moments friends are acquaintances, messages delivered by them can be more convincing."
Well, it sounds true. However, it's just because of friends are acquintances that people tend not to share posts frequently. Events shared by an organization will not go further from people who are in it.
There exist traditional channels to spread information like posters and banners around the campus, and campaigns on the Student Square. They have low exposure rates and cover just a small portion of students.

Insight
The ideal product should provide the most frequently requested information and filter out others. The product should display the information in a clear way which assist students in the just scenario.

#2. User Research on Our Existing Products

Old QSC Mobile is not satisfying anymore.

QSC team has been dedicating itself to building a mobile solution to the problems of cumbersome information. Before I joined, the former designers and engineers developed QSC Mobile. It was designed to be a one-stop shop of the most important information.
We interviewed our typical users, sent out surveys, organized focus group and read the reviews on APP Store, through which we found strong reasons to redesign QSC Mobile.

Insight
In a brand new design, Mobile should be easier and more intuitive to use. A new hierarchy should let users focus on the most important information in different scenarios.

Mobilality drives product merge.

In 2014, QSC had four main products:
Share: an online platform for sharing all kinds of resources.
Notice: an online platform announcing on-campus activities and official notifications.
Box: a service providing lightweight cloud storage. Users could download file anywhere with a passcode.
Mobile: previously introduced as an integration of the schedule of classes, exams, buses as well as grade books.

Among them, Mobile was the most popular one, while, Notice had pitifully few users. Why Notice was not well accepted when students felt isolated from on-campus events? Except from the fact that effort put into operation and promotion was less, interviews explained to us that students felt troublesome to take out their laptop and visit the website. And it was lighter and far more natural to check the events using smartphones.

Insight
We should re-research students' needs, generate typical scenarios, and based on them, design either mobile products or cross-platform products.

#3. Competitive Analysis

What's our core competence from others?

There are various products on the market who share the same features. Among them, Super Timetable and My ZJU are two strong representatives.

Insight
After detailed research and competitative analysis, we were confident to keep our core competence - we are the only official APP and have the only access to all official data - and meanwhile, improved our design and information hierachy to win the users.
